# **🐉 The Primal Wyrms**

*The Ancestral Progenitors of Dragonkind*

## **Core Belief**

The kobolds and dragonborn do not see dragons merely as beasts, but as living echoes of the **First Brood**—six primordial wyrms that embodied the raw forces of creation. These beings, called the **Primal Wyrms**, are not worshiped as distant gods but revered as **ancestral progenitors**. Their essence is believed to still flow in draconic bloodlines, sleeping in the marrow of every scale.

## **Theology & Myths**

* The **Primal Wyrms** hatched from the World-Egg before the Sundering, each embodying a primal source of existence.

* They circled the newborn world, carving rivers, raising mountains, kindling suns, and weaving magic.

* When their conflict shattered the balance, they entered a deep slumber beneath sky, stone, and sea—leaving behind dragons, dragonborn, and kobolds as their inheritors.

* The slumber of true dragons is tied to the same fate; awakening one is seen as a cosmic omen.

## **The Six Progenitors**

**Solkara, the Flame-Wyrm of the Sun**

* **Primal Source:** Fire / Sunlight

* **Aspects:** Purification, destruction, renewal, glory

* **Symbol:** A golden coil of flame devouring its own tail  
* **Metallic echoes:** Gold & Brass dragons (noble fire, renewal, glory).

* **Chromatic echoes:** Red & White dragons (destructive fire, consumption).

* **Notes:** Both Bahamut and Tiamat are seen as Solkara’s “children of fire” — Bahamut embodying the cleansing flame, Tiamat the consuming blaze.  Bahamut and Tiamat are considered rival “children of Solkara,” explaining why both tie to fire/sun in different aspects.

**Lunareth, the Pale Serpent of the Moon**

* **Primal Source:** Moonlight / Cycles

* **Aspects:** Prophecy, transformation, shadow, deception

* **Symbol:** A silver serpent biting a crescent moon  
* **Metallic echoes:** Silver dragons (prophecy, guardianship of cycles).

* **Chromatic echoes:** Blue & Black dragons (shadow, secrets, hunger).

* **Notes:** Patron of kobold seers and assassins alike. Associated with the cycle of egg, hatchling, and death.  Lunareth’s duality explains why moon-serpents can be seen as wise guides *or* sinister deceivers.

**Stellara, the Star-Eyed Dragon**

* **Primal Source:** Stars / Arcane Spark

* **Aspects:** Magic, fate, possibility, wonder

* **Symbol:** A dragon’s eye filled with constellations  
* **Metallic echoes:** Brass & Bronze dragons (curiosity, lore-keeping, wanderlust).

* **Chromatic echoes:** Green dragons (arcane corruption, destiny twisted into tyranny).

* **Notes:** Said to have shed sparks that became gemstones and the first spells.  Stellara explains why many dragons—good and evil—are bound up with magic.

**Thamros, the Earthfather**

* **Primal Source:** Earth / Stone

* **Aspects:** Strength, foundation, guardianship, secrets

* **Symbol:** A dragon’s claw clutching a mountain  
* **Metallic echoes:** Bronze & Copper dragons (protectors, guardians of the deep places).

* **Chromatic echoes:** Black dragons (corruption of earth, decay, swamp-lords).

* **Notes:** Protector of warrens, patron of dragonborn fortresses. Kobold miners whisper prayers to him before every dig.  Seen as “the root” of dragonkind — sturdy, secret-keeping, immovable.

**Aelion, the Sky-Wind**

* **Primal Source:** Sky / Air

* **Aspects:** Freedom, storms, rebellion, inspiration

* **Symbol:** A storm-wreathed dragon in flight  
* **Metallic echoes:** Brass & Silver dragons (freedom, inspiration).

* **Chromatic echoes:** Blue dragons (storm, tyranny through lightning).  
* **Notes:** Favored by dragonborn wanderers and outcasts, kobold scouts, and those who dare challenge tradition.  Aelion’s children are both liberators and storm-lords, depending on their nature.

**Ozythra, the Sea-Mother**

* **Primal Source:** Ocean / Water

* **Aspects:** Depth, hunger, fertility, eternity

* **Symbol:** A coiled sea-serpent encircling a pearl  
* **Metallic echoes:** Bronze & Gold dragons (seafaring guardians, eternal endurance).

* **Chromatic echoes:** Black & Green dragons (hunger, poisonous tides, endless drowning).  
* **Notes:** Worshiped by coastal kobolds and dragonborn enclaves. Both feared and adored—her tides are life and death together.  Ozythra explains the pull between nurturing fertility and devouring depths.

## **Practices & Clergy**

* **Shrines**: Each warren or dragonborn hall maintains six effigies, painted in the colors of their progenitors.

* **Clergy**: Called **Scalebinders**, they are chosen not by birth but by a vision or omen—usually an unusual egg, a strange marking, or a dream of the First Brood.

* **Rituals**:

  * **Egg Blessings** – newborn eggs are placed before six braziers: coal (Sun), moonwater (Moon), starlight crystals (Stars), stone dust (Earth), incense smoke (Sky), and saltwater (Sea).

  * **Blood Oaths** – warriors cut their palms on obsidian shards, offering drops to the six, binding their oaths to dragonkind.

  * **Dream-Trances** – seers inhale special smoke to speak with the “voices of the Brood.”

## **⚖️ Cultural Explanation and Impact**

* **Kobolds** see themselves as the *truest heirs*—faithful keepers of the Brood’s memory. They believe their servitude to dragons is a sacred covenant.

* **Dragonborn** view themselves as the *noble inheritors*—manifest flesh of the Brood. To them, kobolds are dutiful but lesser kin.

* **Schisms**: Some sects elevate Bahamut (as Solkara’s reborn flame) or Tiamat (as Lunareth’s shadow made flesh) above the rest, causing rifts in worship.

* **Kobolds** see chromatic vs. metallic not as “good vs. evil,” but as **imbalances in the echoes** of their progenitors.

* **Dragonborn** often interpret themselves along the old 5e “noble metallic vs. brutal chromatic” line — but in the Primal Wyrm faith, both are valid children of the Brood.

* To outsiders, this faith helps explain why dragons in myths are both heroic saviors and world-destroying terrors: they are shards of the same six primal forces.

## **Domains & Gameplay Hooks**

* **Cleric Domains:** Light (Sun), Trickery (Moon), Arcana (Stars), Forge (Earth), Tempest (Sky), Nature (Sea).

* **Paladin Oaths:** Oath of the Ancients (Stellara), Oath of Vengeance (Lunareth or Solkara), Oath of Devotion (Bahamut/dragonborn sects).

* **Warlock Patrons:** Dragon progenitors, framed as ancient dream-beings still whispering in slumber.
