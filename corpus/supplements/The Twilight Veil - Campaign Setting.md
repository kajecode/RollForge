# The Twilight Veil

## Campaign Setting

### The Pantheon of the Twilight Veil

#### Aurion, The Dawn's Light

* **Domains:** Light, Life, Protection  
* **Symbol:** A golden sunburst with radiant rays  
* **Personality:** Aurion embodies hope and perseverance, standing as the first and brightest bulwark against the shadow. His presence inspires courage in the face of fear.  
* **Clergy and Practices:** Followers honor Aurion with grand sunrise ceremonies, invoking his blessings in times of strife. Temples serve as sanctuaries of light and warmth.

---

#### Selanwë, Keeper of Secrets, The Moon-Maiden

* **Domains:** Hidden Knowledge, Twilight, Arcana  
* **Symbol:** A crescent moon entwined with silver and shadow  
* **Personality:** Selanwë, the Moon-Maiden, weaves truths into the fabric of twilight, guiding seekers through the mysteries of shadow and light. She is a goddess of duality, balance, and wisdom.  
* **Clergy and Practices:** Her followers are scholars and mystics, performing moonlit rituals and seeking truths veiled by shadow. Her priests often serve as interpreters of prophecy.

---

#### Thalyssia, The Everbloom, Verdant Mother

* **Domains:** Nature, Agriculture, Renewal  
* **Symbol:** A flourishing tree entwined with golden vines  
* **Personality:** Thalyssia governs the natural world, embodying its cycles of growth, harvest, and decay. She is both nurturing and unyielding, the steward of nature’s balance.  
* **Clergy and Practices:** Her priests cultivate sacred groves and protect them from corruption. Seasonal festivals honor her influence over the land’s bounty.

---

#### Durn, The Flameforged

* **Domains:** War, Forge, Strength  
* **Symbol:** A hammer wreathed in flames  
* **Personality:** Durn represents the perseverance of creation, forging strength through fire and adversity. He inspires both craftsmen and warriors.  
* **Clergy and Practices:** Temples of Durn double as forges, where weapons are blessed and battles are strategized. His followers see struggle as a path to refinement.

---

#### Nytheris, Whisperer of Fate

* **Domains:** Lost Knowledge, Death, Fate  
* **Symbol:** A raven perched atop a crescent moon  
* **Personality:** Nytheris weaves the threads of destiny and ensures the natural order of death. She opposes undeath and those who defy fate.  
* **Clergy and Practices:** Her priests conduct funerary rites and provide counsel on mortal destinies. Shrines often feature ravens and moonlit pools.

---

#### Maskfather, Keeper of False Truths

* **Domains:** Trickery, Shadow, Illusion  
* **Symbol:** A smiling mask with an open eye upon the brow.  
* **Personality:** A playful, mischievous deity of riddles, shadows, and cunning. His worshippers never agree on his form or nature — sometimes he is a sly bard, sometimes a veiled widow, sometimes a stormrider or fox-spirit. Most treat him as a harmless trickster, a patron of gamblers, thieves, and jesters.  
* **Clergy and Practices:**  Shrines appear in marketplaces, thieves’ dens, and hidden crossroads; Rituals involve wagers, illusions, and masked dances; Worship is informal and diverse — some pray for luck, others for mischief, still others for protection from deceit.  
* **Aliases & Regional Avatars:**  
  * *Maskfather* (Eryndor, Shimmering Expanse) — a flamboyant bard who blesses gamblers.  
  * *The Twilight Widow* (Verdant Veil) — a veiled woman who ensnares liars and oathbreakers.  
  * *The Frost Fox* (Thrymhold) — a silver-eyed fox spirit who tells riddles in winter storms.  
  * *The Masked Arcanist* (Aetheris) — a faceless mage of illusions, invoked by rogue scholars.  
  * *The Oathbreaker’s Shadow* (Stonereach Highlands) — a giant-boned oath-spirit blamed for broken vows.  
  * *The Painted Spider* (Jade Canopy) — an Anari dancer weaving silk illusions.  
  * *Ashmask / The Broken Sky* (Stormhollow Plains) — a stormrider whose cracked mask gleams with lightning.  
* **Role in Worship:**  
   Though scattered and inconsistent, these folk-beliefs create a patchwork of devotion across the Veil. Most mortals assume these are distinct spirits. Few suspect that all masks belong to a single, ancient god.

#### **🌑 DM-Only Entry: Siorath, Keeper of False Truths**

**Domains:** Trickery, Shadow, Illusion  
 **True Symbol:** A cracked mask of shadow and light, with a closed eye at its center.  
 **Personality:** Once believed slain or forgotten after the Sundering, Siorath has never truly died. He is patient and calculating, weaving himself into illusions, whispers, and masks. His avatars slip between male, female, and ambiguous forms, making him impossible to pin down. He thrives when mortals doubt, when truth collapses into contradiction, and when faith fractures.

**Clergy and Practices:**

* True cultists wear masks split between male and female aspects, embodying his shifting face.

* Their rituals involve stripping away illusions to expose truths that destroy, or drowning victims in lies that consume them.

* His shrines are concealed within ruins, beneath false façades, or hidden in the shadows of other temples.

**Goals:**

* To unravel trust in gods and mortals alike, leaving him the sole certainty in a world of falsehood.

* To corrupt leylines and shrines, seeding his name into the foundations of divine power.

* To build networks of thieves, spies, and assassins as his agents, hidden even from themselves.

**Aliases (known only to cultists):** The Maskfather, The Truth-Lie, The Whispering Mask, Ashmask, The Twilight Widow, The Frost Fox, The Painted Spider, The Oathbreaker’s Shadow. All are masks of one god: Siorath.

---

#### Ravok, The Hungering Abyss

* **Domains:** Destruction, Murder, Chaos  
* **Symbol:** A black spiral descending into a pit  
* **Personality:** Ravok embodies entropy and annihilation, worshipped by those who seek to hasten the world’s end or revel in destruction.  
* **Clergy and Practices:** His cults often operate in the shadows, causing chaos and devastation. Shrines are crude, adorned with symbols of decay and ruin.

---

#### Thalorra, The Silent Flame

* **Domains:** Forge, Knowledge, Mystery  
* **Symbol:** A single flickering candle  
* **Personality:** Thalorra represents the quiet pursuit of perfection through innovation and discipline. She inspires inventors, craftsmen, and keepers of lore.  
* **Clergy and Practices:** Worship of Thalorra focuses on invention and preservation. Eternal flames in her honor burn in secretive workshops and grand libraries.

---

#### Karveth, The Roaring Storm

* **Domains:** Tempest, Freedom, Chaos  
* **Symbol:** A stormcloud with a lightning bolt striking water  
* **Personality:** Karveth embodies the untamed forces of nature and the liberation they bring. He inspires sailors, rebels, and those who live freely.  
* **Clergy and Practices:** His rites involve storm-dancing and offering tokens to the sea and wind. His followers thrive on chaos and change.

### Divine Alignment and Themes

* **The Luminous Trinity (Aurion, Selanwë, Thalyssia):** Representing light, wisdom, and life, they embody the forces working to maintain balance and oppose shadow.  
* **The Shadowed Veil (Siorath, Ravok, Nytheris):** These deities challenge or subvert the balance, influencing the rise of shadow and destruction.  
* **The Architects (Durn, Thalorra, Karveth):** Masters of creation, chaos, and discipline, these gods shape the physical and metaphysical worlds.

## Races of Twilight Veil

### **Common Races**

1. **Humans:**  
   * Found throughout kingdoms like **Eryndor**, humans are the dominant race. Their adaptability and ambition have made them leaders in politics, trade, and the resistance against shadow incursions.  
   * **Traits:** Innovation, resilience, and cultural diversity.  
2. **Dwarves:**  
   * The hardy residents of the **Grimfang Mountains**, dwarves are known for their craftsmanship and runic magic. They fiercely protect their strongholds from the shadow’s influence.  
   * **Traits:** Resilience, mastery of metalworking, and runic arts.  
3. **Halflings:**  
   * Thriving in pastoral regions like the **Shimmering Expanse**, halflings avoid shadow-touched areas, maintaining simple lives focused on community and tradition.  
   * **Traits:** Intuition, stealth, and optimism.  
4. **Gnomes:**  
   * Ingenious and curious, gnomes are often found preserving ancient knowledge or experimenting with arcane and mechanical crafts. They play vital roles in resisting shadow corruption.  
   * **Traits:** Creativity, intellect, and a knack for invention.  
5. **Half-Orcs:**  
   * Living on the fringes of civilization, half-orcs excel as warriors and guides, their strength and tenacity proving invaluable against wild and shadow-touched dangers.  
   * **Traits:** Physical endurance, combat prowess, and determination.

---

### **Uncommon Races**

1. **Half-Elves:**  
   * Descendants of the reclusive elves, half-elves are more prevalent in kingdoms like **Eryndor** but often struggle to reconcile their dual heritage.  
   * **Traits:** Adaptability, grace, and diplomatic insight.  
2. **Dragonborn:**  
   * Proud and rare, dragonborn hail from distant lands beyond the Twilight Veil. Their presence often signifies a personal quest or a distant kingdom's involvement.  
   * **Traits:** Nobility, elemental power, and martial discipline.  
3. **Goblinoid (Goblin, Hobgoblin, Bugbear)**  
4. **Kobold**  
5. **Grung**  
6. **Tabaxi**

---

### **Rare Races (DM Approval)**

1. **Hollowborn (formerly Warforged):**  
   * Ancient constructs of metal and wood, created to house lifesparks by a long-lost civilization. Awakened centuries after a cataclysm, they wander the world, seeking purpose and answers to their forgotten past.  
   * **Traits:** Immortal resilience, mysterious origins, and exploratory instincts.  
2. **Changelings:**  
   * Rare shapeshifters often tied to shadow magic, changelings hide their true nature to avoid fear and persecution. Their fluid identities make them excellent infiltrators and spies.  
   * **Traits:** Versatility, deception, and adaptability.  
3. **Anari**  
4. **Titanborn**  
5. **Elf (see below)**

---

### **Rare and Reclusive: Elves**

* **Homeland:** The elves have retreated to **Caladhor, the Veiled Sanctum**, a mystical land of floating islands and ancient magic, accessible only through **Moonlit Gates** during rare celestial alignments.  
* **Legacy in the Material Plane:**  
  * Ancient ruins, relics, and half-elves remain as echoes of their presence.  
  * Elven artifacts, such as the **Compass of the Rangers**, hint at their role in resisting shadow before their withdrawal.  
* **Traits:** Mastery of arcane arts, natural elegance, and timeless wisdom.

### Other Races

#### Goblinoids (Goblin, Hobgoblin, Bugbear)

* **Origin/Region: The Stormhollow Plains and the fringes of the Stonereach Highlands. Their strongholds are often hidden in craggy ravines, reclaimed ruins, or fortified hill-settlements.**

* **Culture: Goblinoids are bound by the Concord of Iron, a tradition that prizes ingenuity, craft, and survival. Their society is clan-based but unusually cooperative for goblins, hobgoblins, and bugbears—each caste has a role (goblins as tinkerers, hobgoblins as strategists, bugbears as enforcers).**

* **Religion: Ancestor veneration; instead of gods, they honor the spirits of inventors, warlords, and explorers, whose souls are believed to guide the living.**

* **Technology: Guardians of Steamtech relics left from the Leyline War—crude firearms (blunderbuss, hand-cannons) and gear-driven machines. They do not export these secrets lightly.**

#### Grung

* **Origin/Region: Marshes of Moonfang Cove and the fetid lagoons of the Jade Canopy.**

* **Culture: Amphibian hunter-gatherers organized by strict color-caste hierarchies. Grung society is both alien and insular, with rigid rules about breeding, food, and labor.**

* **Religion: They appease totemic spirits of water, venom, and the moon, often through song and communal trance rituals. Their worldview is deeply animistic.**

* **Notes: Their alchemical venom is traded in whispers at frontier markets but is highly dangerous to mishandle.**

#### Tabaxi

* **Origin/Region: Roaming clans across the Jade Canopy, especially along the borders of Moonfang Cove and Emberwake.**

* **Culture: Nomadic storytellers, traders, and scouts. They live in caravans of woven canoes and vine-ladders, rarely settling in one place. Their oral tradition preserves lore about the fallen empire beneath the jungle.**

* **Religion: Tabaxi follow Selanwë, the Moon-Maiden, believing her silver light guides their curiosity and wanderlust.**

* **Notes: Seen as mysterious wanderers in cities; often serve as guides through trackless jungle.**

#### Kobolds

* **Origin/Region:** Caves beneath the **Stonereach Highlands** and volcanic tunnels of **Mount Xalthek** in the Jade Canopy.

* **Culture:** Tribal, industrious, and obsessed with draconic lineage. They live in warrens of cunning traps, seeing themselves as the chosen keepers of draconic bloodlines.

* **Religion:** They revere the **Echo of Dragons**—mythic progenitors believed to slumber beneath the world. Shrines often depict a composite of many dragon forms rather than a single deity.

* **Notes:** Some clans follow radical sects that whisper of *Tiamat* or *Baphomet* as living patrons, but these are fringe.

# **The Calendar of the Twilight Veil**

## 🗓 Structure:

* **Each month**: 30 days (3 ten-day weeks).

* **Each season**: 3 months \+ 1 festival day \= 91 days.

* **4 seasons × 91 days \= 364 days**.

* **\+ Thronehold** (Year's End) \= **365 days** total.

# ---

| Season | Festival Day | Months |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| 🌱 Spring | Dawning Bloom | Firstbloom, Greenspire, Seedrise |
| ☀️ Summer | Suncrest | Highsun, Brightfield, Emberreach |
| 🍂 Autumn | Verdant Fall | Goldleaf, Harvestmere, Duskwane |
| ❄️ Winter | Deepnight | Frostveil, Shivershade, Starwend |
| 👑 Year's End | Thronehold | (Day of Peace and Renewal) |

# ---

## **✨ Festival Days Overview:**

* **Dawning Bloom** (Spring Equinox):  
   Welcoming new life and the rebirth of the land. Great planting festivals.

* **Suncrest** (Summer Solstice):  
   Celebrations of light, vitality, and the height of the year's strength.

* **Verdant Fall** (Autumn Equinox):  
   Giving thanks for the harvest and preparing for winter's hardship.

* **Deepnight** (Winter Solstice):  
   A solemn reflection on survival, honoring ancestors and the endurance of the soul.

* **Thronehold** (Year's End):  
   A day of **universal peace**, where even long-standing enemies call a truce.

  * **Nations**, **clans**, and **guilds** observe a day of **parades**, **feasting**, **gift exchanges**, and **treaties renewed**.

  * In many lands, the dawn after Thronehold is seen as an omen:  
     A **bright dawn** means prosperity; a **clouded dawn** means hardship ahead.

---

🧠 Notes for World Flavor:

* **Thronehold** is a *sacred day*, even in war-torn regions — violating the peace of Thronehold is considered a **heinous crime** and a **curse upon one’s bloodline**.

* Even criminals and exiles are often granted safe passage if they swear to return to their hiding on the morrow.

* It is **customary** to settle debts and grievances before Thronehold, or else leave them to "the new year’s judgment."

# Caladhor, The Veiled Sanctum

#### **Overview**

The elves of the Twilight Veil have retreated to **Caladhor**, a distant, mystical land hidden far from the Material Plane's chaos. Accessible only through rare and perilous routes, Caladhor is a sanctuary shaped by ancient magic, where elves preserve their culture and traditions, far removed from mortal affairs.

---

#### **Caladhor: The Mystical Refuge**

1. **Geography and Environment:**  
   * **Floating Archipelago:** Caladhor is a series of lush, floating islands suspended above an endless ocean of shimmering mist. Each island is connected by bridges of glowing moonlight that appear only under starlit skies.  
   * **Verdant Beauty:** The land is covered in forests of silver-barked trees, crystal-clear rivers, and blooming flowers that glow faintly in the twilight.  
   * **Magical Aura:** The air is saturated with ancient, protective magic. Travelers report an overwhelming sense of serenity mixed with awe.  
2. **Access to Caladhor:**  
   * The **Moonlit Gates**, ancient portals hidden in the deepest forests of the Material Plane, are the only way to reach Caladhor. The gates activate only during rare celestial alignments, and only those with elven blood or extraordinary willpower can pass through.  
   * These gates are fiercely protected by elven guardians and wards that confound intruders.

---

#### **Culture of the Elves in Caladhor**

1. **Isolationism:**  
   * The elves of Caladhor see themselves as custodians of knowledge, believing the Material Plane has become corrupted by shadow and mortal hubris.  
   * They rarely leave their sanctuary, save for the **Verdant Watchers**, a select group of elven scouts who monitor the Material Plane for threats that might reach Caladhor.  
2. **Timeless Tradition:**  
   * Elves in Caladhor dedicate their lives to art, magic, and philosophy, striving to perfect their craft in service to their ancestors and the natural order.  
   * They have preserved rituals, histories, and relics thought lost to time, making Caladhor a repository of unmatched lore.  
3. **Architecture:**  
   * Elven cities are woven seamlessly into the natural landscape, with crystal spires, glowing groves, and marble halls adorned with living vines and luminescent flora.  
   * The capital city, **Silvarion**, is a breathtaking amalgamation of crystalline structures and ancient trees that form a perfect symbiosis between nature and magic.

---

#### **Connection to the Twilight Veil**

1. **Elven Artifacts and Ruins:**  
   * Artifacts left behind in the Material Plane hint at Caladhor’s wonders, such as enchanted weapons, moonstone jewelry, and fragments of elven texts. These relics inspire adventurers and scholars to seek the elusive homeland.  
2. **Rare Visitors:**  
   * Occasionally, an elf from Caladhor journeys to the Material Plane, often as a diplomat, scholar, or agent of the Verdant Watchers. These individuals are enigmatic, carrying an aura of otherworldly grace and secrecy.  
3. **Legends and Mystery:**  
   * The name "Caladhor" stirs reverence and curiosity among mortals. Some believe the elves guard divine secrets, while others claim Caladhor is merely a myth.  
   * Those who have glimpsed it in visions describe it as a land of eternal twilight, untouched by shadow or time.

---

#### **Hooks and Implications**

1. **Rediscovery:**  
   * A long-lost Moonlit Gate is uncovered, sparking interest from factions eager to breach Caladhor’s defenses or claim its magic for their own.  
2. **Elven Aid:**  
   * In desperate times, the elves may send emissaries or artifacts to help resist a shadowy threat. However, their help always comes with strict terms.  
3. **Cultural Tensions:**  
   * Half-elves or exiled elves may feel torn between their heritage in Caladhor and their lives in the Material Plane, providing rich character development opportunities.
