#  **Stormhollow Plains**

**Geography & Terrain**

* A vast, **windswept expanse** stretching between the Jade Canopy jungles and the Verdant Veil forests.

* Dominated by **rolling grasslands**, jagged **thorn thickets**, and **scarred mesas** that look torn from the earth itself.

* The skies are restless: sudden **tempest squalls** roll in without warning, turning open plains into a deadly crucible of lightning, hail, and wind.

* **Ash-choked gullies** and stretches of blackened stone suggest lingering scars of the **Great Sundering**, where the land was seared by leyline backlash.

---

**Flora & Fauna**

* **Stormhorn Beasts** — bison-like herds with jagged, lightning-scorched horns, said to draw storms in their wake.

* **Thornprowlers** — great cats adapted to spiny thickets, using the terrain as both camouflage and trap.

* **Ashvultures** — carrion birds with ember-red eyes, circling battlefields and storm-wrecked camps.

* Occasional **elemental incursions** (air, lightning, ash) hint at thin veils between planes.

---

**Inhabitants**

* **Nomadic Clans**: Human and half-orc stormriders who track herds, live lean off the land, and believe each storm is a god’s passing judgment.

* **Druidic Circles**: Harsh-weather druids and shamans who mediate between storm spirits and mortal survival.

* **Predatory Raiders**: Bands of exiles, brigands, and beast-riders, exploiting the plains’ unforgiving nature.

---

**Exports & Value**

* **Stormglass**: A crystal-like substance formed where lightning fuses sand, used in weapons, amulets, and arcane foci.

* **Beast-hides & Horns**: Trophy materials from Stormhorns and Thornprowlers, prized for their resilience and beauty.

* **Tempest Lore**: Nomads are hired as storm-guides, trackers, and mercenaries, bringing their survival craft to other realms.

---

**Tone & Hooks**

* The **Stormhollow Plains are a barrier**: not simply wilderness, but a lingering scar of catastrophe that demands respect from all who cross.

* To the Jade Canopy Anari, the Plains are an ever-present threat; to the druids of the Verdant Veil, they are a cursed land best left to wanderers.

* PCs might pass through while traveling between Southwatch and the far jungles, dealing with lightning storms, predator ambushes, or nomadic diplomacy.

#  **Legacy of the Sundering**

**The Sundering’s Scar**

* During the height of the **Leyline War**, the Stormhollow region sat upon a **leyline convergence** — a nexus of wild energy linking the Jade Canopy to the Verdant Veil.

* When the **Great Sundering** shattered the leyline network, this land was struck with unbridled force:

  * The sky split with ceaseless storms.

  * The earth was scoured into thorned wastes and lightning-glass scars.

  * Beasts and plants twisted into harsh survivalist forms.

**Residual Leyline Effect**

* The Stormhollow Plains still hum with unstable ley energy:

  * Lightning storms surge unnaturally strong, often **striking the same places repeatedly** as though drawn to old wounds.

  * **Elemental rifts** sometimes open mid-storm, spilling air and lightning elementals into the mortal realm.

  * Druids whisper of a **slumbering spirit of storm and wrath**, born of the Sundering’s chaos, that lingers beneath the plains.

**Cultural Memory**

* The **Nomadic Clans** call themselves the *Children of the Ash Sky*, claiming their ancestors survived the first Sundering storms and were forever hardened by it.

* They believe each lightning strike is **Aranwe’s Loom unraveling threads**, or sometimes the judgment of other gods who fought in the Leyline War.

* The **Verdant Veil druids** insist the Plains are a wound that should have healed, but has been kept raw by forces unseen — perhaps Flemeth’s coven, perhaps Siorath’s shadow.

**Adventuring Hooks**

* **Leyline Remnants:** The PCs may discover that a **leyline node** lies buried beneath Stormhollow, still cracked and unstable, threatening further rupture.

* **Storm-Spirit’s Bargain:** A tempest elemental (or proto-titan) offers power in exchange for spreading its influence beyond the Plains.

* **Silk and Storm:** An Anari caravan carrying moonwoven silk must cross Stormhollow, but nomads demand tribute and storm-elementals prowl the route.

* **Forgotten Battlefield:** Ancient relics of the Leyline War lie buried beneath fused stormglass, waiting to be unearthed by the daring — or the foolish.

# **⚡ Sundershard**

**Origin:**  
 When the Great Sundering tore open the leyline nexus beneath what is now the Stormhollow Plains, the earth itself fractured. Where lightning struck sand, soil, and stone in the same instant that ley energy ruptured, **Sundershard** was created — a jagged crystal infused with storm and leyline essence.

**Appearance:**

* Jagged shards of translucent stone, colors shifting between **storm-gray, violet, and silver-white**.

* Each piece carries faint arcs of static across its surface, harmless unless struck or handled improperly.

* When broken or cut, its inner core glows like **trapped lightning**.

---

## **Properties**

* **Conductor of Storms.** A weapon forged with Sundershard hums faintly in the wielder’s hand. Once per long rest, the wielder can cause the weapon to deal an extra **1d6 lightning damage** on a hit for 1 minute.

* **Focus of Leylines.** Spellcasters can use a Sundershard as a spellcasting focus. When they cast a spell that deals lightning or thunder damage, they may reroll one damage die, keeping the new result.

* **Unstable Essence.** Working with Sundershard is dangerous — on a natural 1 attack roll or spellcasting check using it, a **burst of static** strikes the user, dealing 1d4 lightning damage per rarity tier (common, uncommon, rare, very rare).

---

## **Rarity Tiers**

* **Stormglass (Common):** Fragile, fused glass-like material; used for charms, arrows, or jewelry. Pretty but weak.

* **Low-Grade Sundershard (Uncommon):** Shards the size of a dagger pommel, suitable for minor weapon or armor inlays.

* **True Sundershard (Rare):** Larger crystals (fist-sized) used to forge swords, staves, or shields with storm-bound properties.

* **Heart of the Storm (Very Rare/Legendary):** A massive crystal, said to form only where the Sundering’s lightning struck deepest. Radiates lightning and thunder, capable of empowering entire ley rituals.

---

## **Cultural Place**

* **Nomads** believe Sundershard holds the “voices of the storm-ancestors.” They carve fragments into talismans for protection in the open plains.

* **Anari Caravans** sometimes trade moonwoven silk for Sundershard to weave into ceremonial garments that spark faintly when worn.

* **Verdant Veil Druids** consider it a cursed reminder of imbalance, but even they prize shards for rituals that balance storm and soil.

---

## **Adventure Hooks**

* **Storm-Chasing:** A fresh ley-storm has unearthed new veins of Sundershard. Dozens of scavengers are racing to claim it before storms (or predators) return.

* **Shattered Weapon:** A PC might inherit a weapon containing Sundershard, only to discover it’s cracked and unstable — requiring them to risk Stormhollow to reforge it.

* **The Heart of the Storm:** Legend says a **monolithic Sundershard crystal** still lies buried beneath Stormhollow, a sleeping titan’s tooth waiting to be awakened.

# **⚔️ Sundershard Items**

## **1\. Stormfang Blade**

*Weapon (longsword, shortsword, or greatsword), Rare (requires attunement)*

A blade forged with a vein of Sundershard running through its core. It thrums faintly when storms are near.

* **Stormbite.** Once per long rest, you may declare Stormbite before making an attack. On a hit, the weapon deals an extra **2d6 lightning damage** and the target must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution saving throw or be **shocked** (unable to take reactions until the start of its next turn).

* **Conductor’s Edge.** If you are struck by lightning damage while wielding the blade, you may use your reaction to reduce the damage by half, the other half absorbed into the weapon. On your next melee hit within 1 minute, that absorbed damage is released in full.

---

## **2\. Thunderweave Cloak**

*Wondrous Item, Uncommon (requires attunement)*

This cloak blends **moonwoven silk** with fine threads of Sundershard dust, creating faint sparks when in motion.

* **Stormstep.** While wearing the cloak, when you take the Dash action you may move through enemy spaces without provoking opportunity attacks once per long rest.

* **Static Ward.** You have resistance to lightning damage while dashing, climbing, or otherwise in motion.

* **Market Rarity.** Nobles in Southwatch are known to pay **400–600 gp** for one of these cloaks purely as a status symbol.

---

## **3\. Heartshard Focus**

*Wondrous Item, Very Rare (requires attunement by a spellcaster)*

A polished Sundershard crystal the size of a heart, mounted in a silver or bone clasp. Said to have been cut from the “true veins” of Stormhollow.

* **Ley Conductor.** When you cast a spell that deals lightning or thunder damage, you may add your proficiency bonus to the damage roll.

* **Stormcall.** Once per long rest, as an action, you may cast *call lightning* (3rd level, DC 15\) without expending a spell slot. If used during an actual storm, you cast it as if using a 5th-level slot.

* **Unstable Surge.** On a natural 1 when casting a spell through the Heartshard, you take **3d6 lightning damage** as the crystal arcs violently.

Value in markets: **3,000–5,000 gp**, though Stormhollow nomads claim no price is worth the risk of disturbing the storm’s heart.
