# The Jade Canopy

## (Southern Jungles of the Twilight Veil)

# Geography

 A steaming, verdant rainforest stretching across the southern reaches of the world. Colossal kapok trees, strangler figs, and moss-draped banyans form a nearly impenetrable canopy. Hidden within are vine-choked ruins of a forgotten empire, their glyphs overrun by lichen and roots. At the jungle’s heart rises Mount Xalthek, a towering peak whose smoke-plumed summit suggests it may still be an active volcano. To the east, the jungle spills into Moonfang Cove, a wide crescent bay studded with islets, reefs, and half-sunken stone causeways — remnants of the old empire.

# Settlements & Enclaves

**1\. Vaelithra (Anari Enclave)**

* **Population:** \~600 (primarily Anari)

* **Location:** Suspended among the upper canopy, woven from living silk bridges and platforms anchored to colossal trees.

* **Known For:** Moonwoven Silk trade, mystical loom-shrines of Aranwë.

* **Details:** The settlement glows with bioluminescent silk lanterns at night, casting an ethereal web of light. Outsiders rarely ascend without invitation.

* **Tone:** Sacred, otherworldly, but wary of strangers.

  ---

**2\. Korrath’s Landing (River-trade outpost)**

* **Population:** \~300 (mixed humans, lizardfolk, jungle orcs)

* **Location:** Along a navigable tributary feeding into Moonfang Cove.

* **Known For:** Trade of hardwoods, reagents, and illicit spice.

* **Details:** A rough-and-tumble trade camp built on stilts above the floodplain. Merchants risk disease, predators, and rival raiders to ply their goods.

* **Tone:** Lawless frontier outpost — half-market, half-den of smugglers.

  ---

**3\. Zathra’s Spire (Ruined watch-temple)**

* **Population:** \~80 (hermit mystics, treasure-hunters)

* **Location:** Atop a vine-snarled ziggurat near the jungle’s center.

* **Known For:** Forbidden relics and prophetic visions.

* **Details:** Once a high temple of the lost empire, now a pilgrimage site for Anari seers. Rumors claim ley energies surge strongest here, sometimes warping reality.

* **Tone:** Dangerous but spiritually potent.

  ---

**4\. Skalath Clutch (Lizardfolk Marsh-hold)**

* **Population:** \~400 (lizardfolk)

* **Location:** Marshland at the edge of Moonfang Cove.

* **Known For:** Alchemical poisons, ritual hunts.

* **Details:** The lizardfolk dwell in reed-woven huts above the brackish waters, honoring ancient totems of scaled gods. They raid passing vessels unless appeased with tribute.

* **Tone:** Fiercely territorial; outsiders tolerated only as prey or tribute-bearers.

  ---

**5\. Isle of Broken Spears (Moonfang Islet stronghold)**

* **Population:** \~150 (warrior-tribes, exiles)

* **Location:** One of the largest islets in the cove, surrounded by jagged reefs and wrecks.

* **Known For:** Raider fleets and mercenary bands.

* **Details:** A lawless stronghold where outcasts, orc warbands, and renegades gather under shifting warlords. At its heart stands a half-collapsed colossus of a forgotten deity.

* **Tone:** Savage, unpredictable, yet a place to hire ruthless allies.

  ---

**6\. Emberwake (Camp on Mount Xalthek’s slopes)**

* **Population:** \~250 (mixed adventurers, miners, outcasts)

* **Location:** Lower slopes of Mount Xalthek.

* **Known For:** Obsidian, volcanic gems, and fire-tempered steel.

* **Details:** A ramshackle mining camp precariously close to the mountain’s lava vents. Rumors of fire-spirits and salamanders haunt the mines.

* **Tone:** Desperate and dangerous — fortune and doom alike found here.

  ---

  # Trade & Exports

* **Moonwoven Silk** (most prized export, uniquely Anari).

* Rare hardwoods and resins.

* Jungle spices and hallucinogenic herbs.

* Obsidian and volcanic gemstones (from Mount Xalthek).

* Traders vanish at Moonfang Cove — is it pirates, lizardfolk, or something darker beneath the waves?

* Rumors say Mount Xalthek is waking, and Emberwake’s miners have unearthed something they shouldn’t have.

* Zathra’s Spire whispers prophecies tied to the Leyline War — but demands a steep price in blood or sanity.

# **Jungle Orcs of the Jade Canopy**

### **Physique & Culture**

* **Appearance:** Slightly leaner and more agile than their mountain or plains kin, with dark green to umber skin tones that blend with the rainforest canopy. They wear bone piercings, feathered totems, and tattoos made with hallucinogenic plant dyes.

* **Way of Life:** Semi-nomadic hunters and foragers, moving between seasonal camps. They live in woven huts built into trees or on raised stilts above jungle floods. Warbands are small but cunning, relying on ambush, traps, and poison rather than brute-force charges.

* **Social Structure:** Each tribe is led by a **War-Shaman,** who serves as both priest and leader. These leaders are chosen through vision quests involving sacred herbs and trials in the deep jungle.

---

### **Religion & Spiritual Beliefs**

The Jungle Orcs diverge sharply from the more martial, conquest-driven orc religions of other lands. They are animists, revering **spirits of the jungle and volcano**:

* **Karveth, the Roaring Storm** (from the Twilight Veil pantheon) is their patron of freedom, tempest, and chaos. They see his storms as both a gift and a trial.

* **Thalyssia, the Everbloom,** is acknowledged, but only as a distant “Mother of Green” — jungle orcs believe her favor belongs more to the Anari and druids than to them.

* They also worship **volcanic and predatory spirits**, believed to be fragments of the ancient empire’s fallen gods. These are appeased through **blood offerings** (animal sacrifices, sometimes captured foes).

**Totemic Practices:** Each tribe has an animal totem — jaguar, serpent, horned toad, harpy eagle — which guides hunting rituals and warfare. Warriors often don masks or paint to embody their totem before battle.

---

### **Distinctions from “Regular Orcs”**

* **Strategy:** Jungle Orcs are ambushers and guerrillas. They value patience, stealth, and traps — unlike plains orcs who glorify brute raids.

* **Magic:** War-Shamans wield poisoncraft, spirit-magic, and weather-calling, rather than warlike blood-runes.

* **Society:** Less patriarchal warbands, more communal tribes. Both men and women serve as hunters and warriors; shamans may be any gender.

* **Reputation:** To outsiders, they’re seen as terrifying, whispering from the shadows, striking with poisoned darts before vanishing. Traders fear them as much as they do the lizardfolk.

---

### **Example Tribes**

* **The Emberfangs** — live near Mount Xalthek, offering sacrifices to the volcano spirits.

* **The Serpent Coils** — use hallucinogenic poisons, feared for “dream-hunts” where their prey dies in madness.

* **The Skyfangs** — cliff-dwelling orcs who leap from trees and cliffs with glider-wings made of hide and silk.
