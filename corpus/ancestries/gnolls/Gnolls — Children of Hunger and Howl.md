# **Gnolls — Children of Hunger and Howl**

### **Origins & Homeland**

Gnolls are hyena-folk who prowl the **windswept Stonereach Highlands**, stalk the **storm-scarred plains of Stormhollow**, and range into the **fringes of the Jade Canopy**. They thrive in places where survival is harshest, embodying endurance, ferocity, and an untamed spirit. Though long feared as raiders, gnolls are more than beasts — they are hunters, survivors, and seekers of freedom.

---

### **Culture & Society**

* **Clans of the Hunt.** Gnolls live in packs led by dominant matriarchs or war-leaders, but leadership is always contested and proven by deed.

* **Nomadic Hunters.** They move with herds or storms, living lean and fierce. Their camps are crude but mobile, leaving little trace when abandoned.

* **Strength & Loyalty.** Pack-bonds are sacred. Betrayal is rare, but rivals within the pack test each other constantly.

* **Feast & Famine.** Scarcity defines gnoll life. They celebrate abundance with wild feasts, believing sharing food affirms loyalty to the pack.

---

### **Religion & Belief**

* **Totems of Fang & Storm.** Many gnoll clans venerate **Karveth, the Roaring Storm**, seeing his lightning as both fury and freedom.

* **Whispers of Nytheris.** Some honor **Nytheris, Whisperer of Fate**, associating her ravens with death that follows every hunt.

* **Animist Packs.** In the Jade Canopy, gnolls sometimes adopt jungle spirits or volcano gods into their totems, blending them with their stormborn heritage.

* **Rejected Myths.** While outsiders associate gnolls with demonic hunger (e.g. Ravok), most clans despise such corruption, seeing it as a mockery of true survival.

---

### **Relations with Others**

* **Highlanders & Titanborn.** Rivalry and respect; gnolls compete for the same harsh lands, often clashing with titanborn mercenaries or dwarves.

* **Stormhollow Nomads.** Sometimes allies, sometimes rivals — gnolls share storm-feast traditions with the “Children of the Ash Sky.”

* **Jade Canopy Orcs & Lizardfolk.** Competition is fierce, but intermingling is not unknown; some half-blood packs exist on the jungle edge.

* **Human Kingdoms.** Feared as raiders, though mercenary packs occasionally serve kings or councils in desperate wars.

---

## **Gnoll Traits (5e, Twilight Veil Variant)**

**Ability Score Increase.** Increase one ability score by \+2 and another by \+1, or three by \+1 each.

**Age.** Gnolls mature by 12 and rarely live past 70\.

**Alignment.** Most gnolls lean chaotic, bound to instinct and freedom, though pack-law gives some structure.

**Size.** Medium (6–7½ ft., 200–300 lbs.).

**Speed.** 30 ft.

**Darkvision.** 60 ft.

**Rampage.** Once on your turn, when you reduce a creature to 0 hit points with a melee attack, you can move up to half your speed and make one bite attack as a bonus action.

**Bite.** Your bite is a natural weapon that deals 1d6 \+ Strength modifier piercing damage.

**Pack Hunter.** You have advantage on attack rolls against a creature if at least one of your allies is within 5 feet of the creature and not incapacitated.

**Ferocious Endurance.** Once per long rest, when you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead.

**Languages.** Common, Gnoll.

---

## **Gnoll Bloodlines — Packs of Fang and Storm**

When gnolls name themselves, they declare both their **pack** and their **bloodline**. Bloodlines tie gnolls to the lands they hunt and the storms that shape them.

### **⚡ Stormfang Gnolls (Highlands)**

Born of the thunder-peaks of the Stonereach Highlands, Stormfang gnolls see themselves as lightning given flesh.

* **Stormborn Strike.** Once per long rest, when you hit with a melee attack, deal an extra 1d6 lightning damage.

* **Highland Resilience.** You have advantage on saving throws against being knocked prone.

* **Temperament.** Proud, tempestuous, eager for challenges of strength.

---

### **🔥 Ashfang Gnolls (Stormhollow)**

Forged in the Sundering scars of the Stormhollow Plains, Ashfang gnolls are scarred survivors who thrive in chaos.

* **Ash-Scarred Endurance.** When you use *Ferocious Endurance*, you also gain temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.

* **Scavenger’s Instinct.** You gain proficiency in Survival. If already proficient, double your proficiency bonus.

* **Temperament.** Hardened, fatalistic, and cunning — they laugh at death, for they have stared it down since birth.

---

### **🌑 Shadowfang Gnolls (Canopy)**

At home in the Jade Canopy’s dense jungles, Shadowfang gnolls strike from darkness with eerie precision.

* **Shadow Hunter.** You can attempt to Hide as a bonus action when in dim light or darkness.

* **Jungle Stalker.** You gain proficiency in Stealth. If already proficient, you have advantage on Stealth checks in jungle or swamp terrain.

* **Temperament.** Silent, ritualistic, and unsettling — their laughter echoes like spirits in the dark.

---

### **Roleplay Notes**

* **Stormfangs** boast of thunder-born hunts, often challenging others to feats of strength.

* **Ashfangs** believe survival itself is sacred, hoarding stormglass talismans from Stormhollow.

* **Shadowfangs** whisper of Nytheris and jungle spirits, blending hyena-hunger with shamanic rites.

* Adventuring gnolls may embrace or reject their bloodline — forging a new pack among strangers, or carrying the howl of their ancestors into foreign lands.
