# **🌙 Caladhari (Elves of the Veiled Sanctum)**

### **Physiology**

* Tall, slender, and graceful, with angular features and luminous eyes that reflect starlight.

* Skin tones range from pale moonlight silver to deep twilight bronze; hair often flows in shades of white, gold, or midnight blue.

* Their presence feels ethereal, as though part of them lingers in another world.

### **Culture**

* **Retreat into Caladhor.** The majority of the elven race has withdrawn into **Caladhor, the Veiled Sanctum**, a realm beyond moonlit gates, leaving behind only ruins and relics.

* **Memory-Keepers.** The few who remain often serve as guardians of old shrines, scholars of lost lore, or wandering emissaries bound by ancient oaths.

* **Outlook.** They view the world as a fading song, beautiful yet sorrowful, and treat mortals with a mixture of reverence and detachment.

### **Faith**

* Most revere **Selanwë, the Moon-Maiden**, or **Thalyssia, the Everbloom**, though some cling to memories of older, half-forgotten gods.

* Worship is often private, marked by starlit vigils, moonlit dances, and offerings of woven silver.

### **Society**

* Caladhari seldom form communities outside Caladhor. A lone Caladhari in the world is usually an envoy, oathbound guardian, or wanderer drawn by visions.

* Their isolation has made them objects of myth — admired, feared, or mistrusted.

---

### **Caladhari Traits (5e)**

* **Ability Score Increase.** \+2 Dexterity.

* **Age.** Mature at \~100 years, lifespan of 700+.

* **Alignment.** Tend toward neutral alignments, seeking balance and preservation.

* **Size.** Medium (5–6½ feet tall).

* **Speed.** 30 ft.

* **Darkvision.** 60 ft.

* **Fey Ancestry.** Advantage on saving throws vs. being charmed; immune to magical sleep.

* **Veil Step.** Once per long rest, you can teleport up to 30 ft. to an unoccupied space you can see as a bonus action.

* **Languages.** Common and Caladhari (Elvish).

**Lore Note:** To most mortals, Caladhari are myth. Their ruins and relics are more common than their living kind. Those rare few encountered are treated with awe or suspicion.
