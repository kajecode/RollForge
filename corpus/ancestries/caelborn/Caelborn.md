# **🌒 Caelborn (Children of Twilight)**

### **Physiology**

* Slightly taller and more lithe than humans, but sturdier than their Caladhari kin.

* Hair and eyes often carry faint luminescence, though muted compared to full-blooded elves.

* They move with quiet grace, yet their features carry both mortal vigor and elven melancholy.

### **Culture**

* **Between Worlds.** Caelborn are the descendants of those who refused to follow the Caladhari retreat, born of unions with humankind.

* **Adaptive & Restless.** They thrive as diplomats, adventurers, and seekers, often wandering beyond their homelands.

* **Mixed Heritage.** While some revere their Caladhari bloodline, others reject it, forging wholly new cultural identities.

### **Faith**

* Caelborn often follow local human faiths, though some keep fragments of elven traditions.

* Many venerate **Selanwë** or **Aurion** as guides through their liminal existence, seeing themselves as bridges between light and shadow.

### **Society**

* Widely found in Eryndor and its borderlands, where their dual heritage makes them natural mediators.

* They are respected for their versatility but sometimes regarded with suspicion — seen as reminders of elven abandonment.

* Their adaptability allows them to blend into many cultures, though few Caelborn claim a true homeland of their own.

---

### **Caelborn Traits (5e)**

* **Ability Score Increase.** \+2 Charisma, and \+1 to one other ability of your choice.

* **Age.** Mature like humans, live up to \~180 years.

* **Alignment.** Tend toward neutrality, but highly adaptable.

* **Size.** Medium.

* **Speed.** 30 ft.

* **Darkvision.** 60 ft.

* **Fey Heritage.** Advantage on saving throws vs. being charmed, and once per long rest you may automatically succeed on a saving throw against a single charm or illusion effect.

* **Liminal Insight.** Proficiency in one of the following: Insight, Persuasion, or Stealth.

* **Languages.** Common, Caladhari (Elvish), and one of your choice.

**Lore Note:** The Caelborn are viewed with mixed feelings. Some revere them as bridges to the lost wisdom of the elves, while others scorn them as reminders of Caladhari abandonment.
