# **🕷️ The Anari — Spiderfolk of the Jade Canopy**

* **Physiology:**

  * Tall, slender humanoids with eight black, chitinous eyes and delicate mandibles.

  * Skin tones range from obsidian black to earthen brown, often patterned like spider markings.

  * Hands and feet end in claw-like digits for climbing; can exude silken thread from fingertips.

* **Culture:**

  * **Weavers’ Houses:** Each family weaves massive silken treetop halls that sway high in the jungle canopy.

  * **Communal Looms:** Silk production is sacred — all thread is considered a gift from their goddess.

  * **Diplomacy:** Pragmatic traders, but distrusted by outsiders who see them as eerie or sinister.

* **Religion:**

  * **Deity:** *Aranwe the Loom-Mother* — a spider goddess of creation, patience, and hidden snares.

  * *Aspects:*

    * **The Loom of Fate** — all destinies woven together.

    * **The Fanged Maw** — destruction, punishment for arrogance.

  * Worship involves offerings of woven silk, trance-dances in treetop webs, and sometimes sacrificial prey.

  * Outsiders whisper comparisons to Lloth, but the Anari see Aranwe as a **dual goddess**: nurturer of creation and predator of folly.

* **Exports:**

  * **Moonwoven Silk** — stronger than steel but feather-light, resistant to rot, and shimmers with iridescent patterns. Highly coveted by Southwatch merchants.

  * Poisoncraft, exotic dyes, rare medicines.

# **🕷️ Anari (Spiderfolk) — Playable 5e Race**

**Ability Score Increase:** You may increase one ability score by **\+2** and another by **\+1**, or three different scores by **\+1** each.  
 *(Matches standard lineage flexibility.)*

**Size:** Medium (Anari range from 5½ to 6½ feet tall, slender but wiry).  
 **Speed:** 30 ft. walking, 30 ft. climbing (see *Spider Climb* below).

---

### **🕸️ Racial Traits**

**Darkvision.** You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light.

**Silken Thread.** As an action, you can produce up to 30 feet of strong, adhesive silk rope from your fingertips.

* This silk functions as hempen rope but is flammable.

* You can create an amount equal to 10 × your proficiency bonus per long rest.

**Spider Climb.** You have a climbing speed equal to your walking speed. At 3rd level, you can move up, down, and across vertical surfaces and upside down along ceilings, while leaving your hands free.

**Web Sense.** While in contact with a web you created, you know the exact location of any other creature in contact with the same web.

**Venomous Bite.** You can use your mandibles to deliver a natural attack:

* Melee Weapon Attack: proficiency \+ Dex or Str modifier to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.

* On a hit, the target takes 1d4 piercing damage plus 1d4 poison damage.

* Starting at 5th level, you may force the target to make a Constitution saving throw (DC \= 8 \+ proficiency bonus \+ Con modifier) or be poisoned until the end of your next turn.

* You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

**Languages.** You can speak, read, and write Common and Anari (a chittering tongue of clicks and silken gestures).

---

### **🕷️ Anari Culture Notes (Optional for RP)**

* **Moonwoven Heritage:** All Anari silk is considered sacred; wearing it honors their goddess.

* **Dual Devotion:** Their spider-goddess, *Aranwe the Loom-Mother*, embodies patience and creativity but also predation and judgment.

* **Strangers in the Sunlight:** Most Anari prefer shaded or web-covered spaces, and many outsiders find their mannerisms unsettling.

# **🕷️ Anari Subraces**

---

## **🧵 Weaver Anari (Silkmasters)**

**Silkshaper.** When you use your **Silken Thread** feature, you may also shape the silk into simple objects (rope ladder, net, hammock, bandages). These items last for 24 hours before degrading.  
 **Spider’s Loom.** You can cast *Mending* at will. At 3rd level, you can cast *Entangle* once per long rest. Constitution is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

---

## **🕸️ Hunter Anari (Venomfangs)**

**Keen Predator.** You have proficiency in the Stealth skill.  
 **Potent Venom.** When you use your **Venomous Bite**, the poison damage increases to 1d6. Starting at 5th level, a creature that fails its saving throw is poisoned for 1 minute (save ends at the end of each of its turns). You can use this enhanced venom a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

---

## **🔮 Oracle Anari (Webseers)**

Considered closest to *Aranwe the Loom-Mother*, these mystics weave strands of prophecy.

**Loom of Fate.** You gain proficiency in the Insight skill.  
 **Prophetic Trance.** Once per long rest, when you finish a short rest, you may enter a meditative trance with your woven webbing. Roll a d20. Until the end of your next long rest, you may replace one attack roll, saving throw, or ability check made by you or a creature you can see within 30 ft. with the number you rolled. You must declare the replacement before the roll is resolved.  
 **Mystic Threads.** You know the *Guidance* cantrip. At 3rd level, you may cast *Augury* once per long rest. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

---

# **🎭 Summary of Subraces**

* **Weaver** \= utility and battlefield control (silk creation, *Entangle*).

* **Hunter** \= combat-focused, stealthy predators with stronger venom.

* **Oracle** \= divination-leaning support, with a “fated roll” mechanic to simulate prophecy.

---

# **🕷️ Design Notes**

* The **Weaver** makes great rogues, artificers, or druids.

* The **Hunter** makes natural rangers, monks, or rogues.

* The **Oracle** makes thematic clerics, warlocks, or divination wizards.

# **🕷️ The Web of Anari Society**

The Anari see their entire people as a **woven web**, with each strand vital to the survival of the whole. Subraces are not castes, but **paths of devotion** chosen (or revealed) during adolescence when a young Anari weaves their first silken tapestry under the eyes of the Loom-Mother’s priesthood. Their gifts determine their role.

---

## **🧵 Weaver Anari (The Loomhands)**

**Role in Society:** Builders, artisans, healers, and keepers of silken tradition.

* Weavers spin the great canopy-halls suspended between the colossal jungle trees.

* They maintain communal “lifethreads” — giant woven bridges and nets that support entire treetop villages.

* Their skill extends to craft and medicine: bandages, poultices, nets, and traps woven from enchanted silk.

**View of Aranwe:** To Weavers, Aranwe is the **Nurturing Mother**, patient and creative, who gifts silk to sustain and protect.

---

## **🕸️ Hunter Anari (The Fangborn)**

**Role in Society:** Scouts, hunters, and silent guardians of the canopy.

* They stalk prey both animal and intruder, using stealth and venom to protect their people.

* Hunters often lead expeditions beyond the Jade Canopy, bartering silk and poisoncraft in foreign markets.

* They are respected but sometimes feared even within their own society, embodying the predatory face of Aranwe.

**View of Aranwe:** To Hunters, Aranwe is the **Fanged Maw**, fierce and unrelenting — the predator that strikes down arrogance and hubris.

---

## **🔮 Oracle Anari (The Webseers)**

**Role in Society:** Mystics, priests, and interpreters of fate.

* Oracles weave *divinatory webs* — complex lattices strung with runes and objects to catch omens in the breeze.

* They oversee rites of passage, marriages, and funerals, claiming to glimpse each soul’s strand in the Loom of Fate.

* Some Oracles wander abroad as vision-seekers, interpreting Aranwe’s will in distant lands.

**View of Aranwe:** To Oracles, Aranwe is the **Loom of Fate**, endlessly weaving destinies, patient but inescapable.

---

# **🕷️ Interweaving Roles**

* **Weavers** provide the silk that sustains life.

* **Hunters** protect and procure what cannot be grown or woven.

* **Oracles** interpret the hidden pattern that binds them all.

Together they form a **triad of balance** within Anari culture, ensuring no single role dominates. Each sees the others as part of the divine whole, though tensions sometimes arise — Hunters see Oracles as too passive, Oracles warn against Hunters’ rashness, and Weavers temper both with practicality.

# **🕸️ Moonwoven Silk**

**Origin:**  
 Spun by the Anari (spiderfolk) of the Jade Canopy, Moonwoven Silk is created when sacred weavers blend natural spider-silk with threads imbued through nightly ritual under Aranwe’s gaze. The process is more **prayer than craft** — each thread carries fragments of song, vision, or prophecy.

**Appearance:**

* Silken threads that shimmer in shifting hues: pale silver, deep violet, or faint blue depending on the moon phase they were spun under.

* Light as gossamer, yet **stronger than steel when woven properly**.

* When seen under moonlight, woven fabric gives the impression of faint, moving patterns — like flowing script or subtle constellations.

---

## **Properties**

* **Weightless Strength.** As durable as chain links, yet lighter than linen.

* **Resonant Thread.** When worn or carried, Moonwoven Silk subtly amplifies emotion and intent; some claim it responds to destiny itself.

* **Sacred Craft.** Only Anari weavers, or those trained by them, know the secret chants to weave it correctly. Mundane attempts fail or create unstable cloth.

---

## **Rarity Tiers**

* **Moonthread (Common):** Thin strands used for embroidery, jewelry, or charms; faint shimmer under moonlight.

* **Moonlace (Uncommon):** Decorative but sturdy fabric, used for cloaks, gloves, and banners; carries faint protective qualities.

* **Moonwoven Cloth (Rare):** Full garments or armor pieces, offering magical resistances and durability.

* **Eclipsed Weave (Very Rare/Legendary):** Made during a lunar eclipse — said to hold prophetic power or even store fragments of dreams.

---

# **🌙 Moonwoven Silk Items**

## **1\. Silverveil Mantle**

*Cloak, Uncommon (requires attunement)*

* Grants **advantage on Stealth checks** made in dim light or darkness.

* Once per long rest, you can activate the cloak to blend with shadows and moonlight for 1 minute, granting **invisibility until you attack or cast a spell**.

* Market value: **400–600 gp**.

---

## **2\. Threads of Harmony**

*Wondrous Item, Rare (requires attunement)*

* Fine gloves woven of Moonwoven Silk.

* While attuned, when you use Bardic Inspiration, you may grant **\+1d4 temporary hit points** to the recipient.

* Once per long rest, you may reroll a Charisma check and take the higher result.

* Market value: **1,200–1,800 gp**.

---

## **3\. Prophet’s Shroud**

*Wondrous Item, Very Rare (requires attunement by a cleric, druid, or wizard)*

* Garment woven only during a lunar eclipse, threads said to be sung into being by Aranwe’s priest-weavers.

* Grants **advantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks** and saving throws against being charmed.

* Once per long rest, you may enter a **prophetic trance** for 1 minute: you can cast *Augury* without components, and the trance provides advantage on your next saving throw.

* Market value: **3,500–5,000 gp**.

---

## **4\. Moonwoven Hauberk**

*Armor (light or medium, typically studded leather or chain shirt), Rare (requires attunement)*

* AC as normal for the base armor, but counts as **weightless** (no Strength requirement, Stealth not disadvantaged).

* Grants **resistance to radiant damage**.

* When reduced to 0 hp once per long rest, the wearer instead drops to 1 hp as threads shimmer with moonlight.

* Market value: **2,000–2,800 gp**.

---

# **Cultural Role**

* Among the Anari, Moonwoven Silk is a **gift of trust** — given to allies, envoys, or chosen heroes.

* Smuggled pieces occasionally appear in Southwatch markets (likely via Rivertide Exchange or Lantern & Loom), but most are jealously guarded by Anari caravans.

* To outsiders, it is both **luxury and legend**, akin to elven cloaks or dwarven mithril.
