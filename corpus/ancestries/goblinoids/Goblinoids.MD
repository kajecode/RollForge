# **Goblinoids — Children of the Concord of Iron**

### **Lore Overview**

Forged in the scars of the Stormhollow and tempered by the high winds of the Stonereach, goblins, hobgoblins, and bugbears united under the **Concord of Iron**. What began as fragile truces became an enduring tradition: every caste has its role, every invention or oath strengthens the whole. Goblinoids are no longer seen solely as raiders or savages; they are survivors, machinists, and strategists, bound to their ancestors and their iron.

* **Goblins**: Tinkerers and innovators, scrappy and inventive.

* **Hobgoblins**: Strategists and tacticians, disciplined leaders.

* **Bugbears**: Enforcers and guardians, looming yet stealthy.

Ancestor veneration replaces divine worship. To a goblinoid, the spirits of past inventors, warlords, and explorers still whisper guidance through iron tokens, ancestral forges, and storm-scarred stones.

## **Goblinoid Traits (5e)**

**Ability Score Increase.** You may increase one ability score by \+2 and another by \+1, or three different scores by \+1 each.

**Age.** Goblinoids mature quickly, reaching adulthood at 12\. Goblins live up to 60 years, hobgoblins around 80, and bugbears up to 100\.

**Alignment.** Goblinoids value survival and cooperation. Hobgoblins lean toward lawful, goblins toward neutral, and bugbears toward neutral or chaotic.

**Size.**

* Goblins: Small (3–4 ft., 40–80 lbs.).

* Hobgoblins: Medium (5–6 ft., 120–180 lbs.).

* Bugbears: Medium (6–7½ ft., 200–350 lbs.).

**Speed.** Your base walking speed is 30 ft. (Goblins may choose 25 ft. but gain 35 ft. climbing speed if desired).

**Darkvision.** 60 ft.

**Languages.** Common and Goblin.

---

### **Concord of Iron Features**

All goblinoids share the following traits:

* **Ingenuity of Iron.** You gain proficiency with one set of artisan’s tools (tinker’s tools, smith’s tools, or alchemist’s supplies).

* **Ancestor’s Whisper.** Once per long rest, you may reroll a failed ability check related to Craft (tools), Strategy (History, Investigation), or Survival (Stealth, Survival, Athletics).

* **Iron Oath.** When you make a saving throw against being frightened, you may add \+1d4. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

## **Goblinoid Subraces**

### **Goblin — The Tinkerers**

**Traits**

* **Nimble Escape.** You can take the Disengage or Hide action as a bonus action on each of your turns.

* **Scrapwork Genius.** You can improvise simple contraptions (e.g., a 10-ft snare line, noise trap, or spark-flint) with 10 minutes of work and appropriate materials. Once per long rest, you may treat such a contraption as granting advantage on a related skill check (e.g., Stealth for a noise trap).

---

### **Hobgoblin — The Strategists**

**Traits**

* **Martial Discipline.** You gain proficiency with two martial weapons of your choice.

* **Tactical Aid.** As a bonus action, you can grant one ally within 30 ft. advantage on their next attack roll against a creature you can both see. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

---

### **Bugbear — The Enforcers**

**Traits**

* **Long-Limbed.** When you make a melee attack on your turn, your reach increases by 5 ft.

* **Silent Stalker.** You gain proficiency in Stealth. When you move at least 20 ft. straight toward a creature before hitting it with a melee attack, you deal an extra 1d6 damage.

---

## **Cultural Notes for Roleplay**

* **Ancestor Shrines.** Carrying an iron token or engraved plate is common; each marks the deeds of a forebear.

* **Iron Oaths.** A goblinoid who breaks an oath risks exile; their name is struck from clan rolls.

* **Clanship.** Goblins, hobgoblins, and bugbears operate as one people. While outsiders often mistake them as divided, within the Concord each role is essential.

* **Storm Memory.** Plains goblinoids test themselves by standing vigil during lightning storms, believing survival marks them as blessed by their ancestors.

* **Relations.** Suspicion remains from humans and dwarves, but goblinoid mercenaries and smiths are increasingly sought after.
